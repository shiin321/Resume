CATCH THE MIDDLE LIGHT GAME!

The project "CATCH THE MIDDLE LIGHT GAME!" was developed to explore the application of assembly language in conjunction with PIC16F84A microcontroller for learning purposes.
The code was adapted following a gaming format and demonstration to enhance understanding of the system's functionality. The project serves as a foundation for further development, potentially evolving into a security surveillance system. However, the creators acknowledge a limited knowledge in this field, leading to potential errors. The primary goal of this project is educational, focusing on the integration of assembly language and the use of PIC16F84A in a unique context. It aims to promote diversity in the application of assembly language and encourage broader utilization of the PIC16F84A microcontroller.

Eco-Roadways: Harnessing Wind and Sun to Power Electric Cars

Currently, a significant concern in the use of electric cars is the charging infrastructure. Many areas still lack sufficient charging stations, posing a challenge for widespread adoption. The issue becomes more severe as the popularity of electric cars continues to grow. However, this problem is being addressed through the development of smart roads that automatically charge vehicles. These intelligent roads utilize rotating blades and solar panels as sources of electricity generation. This innovative solution aims to alleviate the concerns associated with the charging infrastructure, making electric cars more feasible and accessible.

Internship Project : Website for Take a leave of absence

This project involves developing a website for employees to request leave on a daily basis, with an approval or decline process by their supervisors. The leave request will only be considered successful upon approval from the respective supervisor. This website is intended for internal use and is exclusive to a specific company. Additionally, there will be a Human Resources department responsible for managing various employee data within the website.

Final Project : The Artificial Thermometer With RTD Sensor
Controlled By Developed LabVIEW Program

This project aims to present the creation of an automatic temperature measurement tool that measures, analyzes, and controls a system, aiming to simplify the complexity of writing and debugging computer programs. The central control will be facilitated by LabVIEW programming, serving as an intermediary for issuing commands, measuring, analyzing, and controlling the system. The system will utilize RTD/PT100 temperature sensor devices, and as these sensors start operating, data will be automatically transmitted back to the LabVIEW program. Users can conveniently and rapidly view the collected data or graphs in real-time, providing a user-friendly and efficient experience for data analysis and monitoring.
